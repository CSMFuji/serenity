Curated tools and professional resources for common mental health challenges.

Anxiety

5-4-3-2-1 Grounding

A simple sensory technique to anchor yourself when anxiety spikes. Name 5 things you see, 4 you can touch, 3 you hear, 2 you smell, 1 you taste.

Practice with Serenity →
Anxiety

Box Breathing

Used by Navy SEALs and therapists alike. Inhale 4 counts, hold 4, exhale 4, hold 4. Activates the parasympathetic nervous system.

Try a guided session →
Depression

Behavioral Activation

A core CBT technique: when motivation is low, action comes before feeling — not after. Small steps create momentum.

Talk it through →
Stress

Cognitive Reframing

Identify negative thought patterns and challenge them. Ask: is this thought fact or interpretation? What would I tell a friend?

Our Story

Why Serenity exists

Serenity was built by a high school student who believes mental health support shouldn't be gatekept by cost, availability, or stigma. This project grew from a passion for psychology and a simple idea: what if there was a compassionate, always-available space for anyone who just needed to feel heard?


This site is not a replacement for professional therapy. If you're struggling, please reach out to a trusted adult, school counselor, or one of the crisis resources listed here. Serenity is a supplement — a first step, a breathing room, a gentle companion.
